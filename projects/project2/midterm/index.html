A Little Bit about Me

Hello again! This is my new personal website for DART 449. If you want to check out the similarities or differences between then and now, check out my old homepage (yet to appear)!

My name is Lam Ky Anh Do, but I go by Falé. It's easier to remember, and I am currently a third-year Design student at Concordia University!

Born and raised in Ho Chi Minh, Vietnam, I moved to Montreal at the age of 8 for a new adventure with my family, who you can discover more in the previous version of this website. From a very young age, my education was highly academic, meaning I didn't have the chance to explore the world of art.

Nonetheless, I was able to experience it through movies, music and manga! Thus, I wanted to learn more about this wonderful world called Art, and so after graduating from my academic international high school, I took the first step and enrolled into Dawson College's Visual Arts program, where I was able to learn and grasp a better understanding of the art world.

Drawing, photography and sculpture, especially painting and printing were enlightening, interesting and fun! However, I felt that there was something missing, as in it was not what I wanted to do career-wise. After a long reflection, I came to realize that I wanted to not only create for the sake of creating and of selling my artworks. Instead, I wanted my creations to have a purpose that can problem-solve issues, contribute to society and spread joy. Thus, Design was what I was looking for and I enrolled into Concordia University's Design and Computation Art program.

So far, I enjoy what I learn as I continuously discover new things everyday and learn about so many design facets. I learned about the history of design that is constantly growing as time flies by, graphic design, industrial design, book design, web design, computation art and so much more. That said, I am leaning more towards graphic design as it is what piqued my interest in studying Design.

Yet, I would still wish to have a multidisplinary background as a graphic designer. Book design and web design are disciplines I would like to learn more about too but I still need to improve my skills by studying more about them in order to claim the right to call myself a book and web designer. Though I am still exploring, I usually work with Functionality, Aesthetic and Playfulness to design and I look for Joy, Efficiency and Simplicity in my designs. In essence, I aspire to become a designer who spreads positivity and happiness.
