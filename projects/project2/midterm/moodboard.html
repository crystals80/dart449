Inspirational Websites

I plan to make an informative website on endangered turtles. I would like to make my website responsive by playing with both long and parallax scrolling examples, easing transitions and animations. For the design of my website, I imagine it to have a simple and straightforward layout guide the useron a journey learning more about the 5 chosen endangered sea turtles. That said, the user will arrive on the landing page where they have the choice to chose which endangered sea turtles they would like to learn more about, then when they have chosen one path they will have to go through that learning path before coming back to the landing page. However, The user will be able to scroll back and forth using long or parallax scrolling. As for the interactivity, users can get out of this by experiencing a satisfying learning experience with the help of calming yet playful animations and easing transitions.

Kalso: The Story Behind The Original Wellness Shoe

I was inspired by the simplicity of the website and the smooth transition from one element after another. I also like the animations as they were quite simple, however I will not borrow this kind of animation as it is quite sudden popping out the moment I land on the right area to trigger it. Still, if I were to use a similar concept of this popping-out animation, I would reduce the speed to match the rhythm of the calm sea. Otherwise, the colour palette is coherent and simple, and the navigation is straightforward.

This Side of Rice by Jenny Zhang

As for this wonderful website done by Jenny Zhang, the way she demonstrated the content and the visuals of her topic is how I would like to present my topic by simply showing the information and explanations through the parallax scrolling with visual support. Also, the sense of playfulness and style of her illustrations are something I am interested in doing. But I would also like to work with images to emphasize on the need of rescuing endangered sea turtles

Camping in Geversduin, Netherlands for Christmas and New Year

Las but not least, this website inspired me with its playfulness. Intrigued by how the animations and transitions of element depicting the "joyful holiday" with the parallax scrolling, I would like to imitate or replicate this sense of narrative. I also like the fact the website is very minimalist, showing just the bare minimal instructions for user to navigate through the website. I like the idea of including a soundtrack to enhance the experience and the indicator to scroll.
